description Fund Fees Explained

Mutual fund fees typically fall into two main categories: shareholder fees and annual fund operating expenses. Shareholder fees are sales charges or commissions, often called "loads," paid when buying or selling shares. Annual operating expenses, commonly expressed as the Expense Ratio, cover management, administrative, and marketing (12b-1) costs on an ongoing basis.
